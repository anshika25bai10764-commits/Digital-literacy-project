Digital Literacy is the ability to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information using digital technologies safely and ethically. In today's world it's not just a skill — it's your superpower as a student. From researching assignments to building your professional brand online, being digitally literate helps you thrive in academics and beyond.

💡

What is Digital Literacy?

It's a 5-skill framework that every student needs:

  • Find & evaluate credible information online
  • Stay safe and protect your privacy
  • Create content responsibly
  • Collaborate using digital tools
  • Understand digital ethics & rights

Safe Internet Practices

  • Use strong, unique passwords + a manager
  • Enable 2-factor authentication everywhere
  • Don't click suspicious links or attachments
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks; use VPN
  • Check privacy settings on every app
  • Back up important files regularly
🌟

Professional Online Presence

Your online profile is your digital résumé. Recruiters google you before interviews.

  • Use a clear, professional profile photo
  • Keep LinkedIn headline updated with skills
  • Showcase projects on GitHub / portfolio site
  • Use a consistent username across platforms

Email Etiquette

  • Clear subject line — be specific
  • Start with proper greeting (Dear Prof. …)
  • State your purpose in the first line
  • Keep it concise — under 150 words
  • Proofread before hitting Send
  • Avoid slang, emojis, ALL CAPS
  • Never send without a signature block
