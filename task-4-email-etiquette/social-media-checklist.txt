Social Media Checklist for College Students
Do's
* Privacy Awareness: Regularly review and update your privacy settings to control who can see your personal information and posts.
* Professional Networking: Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your skills in areas like Python programming and connect with industry professionals.
* Think Before You Post: Before sharing, consider if the content aligns with your values and how it might be perceived by a future employer or proctor.
* Fact-Check Information: Verify the accuracy of news or academic resources before sharing them to maintain digital integrity.
* Constructive Engagement: Participate in positive communities, such as those focused on PC gaming, self-exploration, or universal human values.
Don'ts
* Policy Violations: Never post content that suggests a violation of university rules or academic integrity.
* Sharing Sensitive Data: Avoid posting private details like your university registration number, phone number, or home address.
* Hostile Behavior: Refrain from engaging in cyberbullying, hate speech, or using hostile language in online debates.
* Venting Professionally: Do not post negative comments about professors, internship coordinators, or specific university staff.
* Oversharing: Avoid posting in the heat of the moment or sharing information that could compromise your personal safety or reputation.
